Product Roadmap

What should a VR Social Network look like? We think it should help people:

  • Make new friends
  • Strengthen existing friendships
  • Socialize more
  • Find and build community

Here's our plan to make that happen.

Your ROVR Profile helps you:

  • Let people know your interests, hobbies, and background in VR and IRL
  • Direct people to other accounts (i.e. Twitter, Discord, Steam, VRChat, etc.)
  • See shared friends, personality traits, and groups

ROVR is privacy-first. You can change or remove your profile information at any time, and change your visibility in your privacy settings.

Your ROVR Guest List introduces you to people who share profile tags, interests, personality traits and mutual friends with you.

As we refine the Guest List, we will reveal and explain its algorithm, which factors in common interests, mutual friends, personality traits, shared groups, and more.

Community Groups help people find or create groups based on shared interests, activities, or existing communities and Discord servers.

Coming soon:

  • Events, which will let you create and join VR events in any VR app/game, see what your friends are up to, and find things to do together. This includes one-off events (like conferences), recurring events (like weekly clubs), LFG (for finding teammates), and spontaneous hangouts.
  • Group matchmaking, to meet other people in a group setting, and to meet new people with your existing friends.
  • A VRChat world and worlds in other Social VR apps.
  • Public API for third-party integrations, so that you can more easily meet, message, and meet up with users across platforms.

How will we make money?

Though ROVR is open-source and open-algorithm, we still need to make money in order to run our servers and feed our developers. We are committed to monetizing ROVR in user-respecting and user-aligned ways. Following the examples of Steam and Discord, we believe a sustainable business model will be a combination of customizations (like stickers, backgrounds, and custom emojis) and power-user features (like additional filters, boosts, and options).
